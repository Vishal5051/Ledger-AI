# GTM (Go‑to‑Market) Strategy

## Target Audience
- **CTOs / Founders** of early‑stage SaaS startups (5‑50 engineers) who manage AI‑augmented development teams.
- **Product Engineering Leads** at AI‑first remote companies that already use tools like Cursor, Claude, GitHub Copilot, and Midjourney.
- **Indie Hackers / Freelance devs** building side‑projects that rely heavily on AI APIs.

## Pain Point Search Queries
- "how to reduce AI tool costs"
- "optimize ChatGPT team subscription"
- "best AI SaaS stack for small teams"
- "AI spend audit"
- "AI tool licensing optimization"

## Where They Hang Out
- Indie Hackers forum threads on AI tooling.
- Reddit communities: r/MachineLearning, r/SideProject, r/IndieHackers.
- Discord servers for AI developer tools (e.g., `/r/ClaudeAI`, `Cursor Community`).
- Product Hunt launch page and newsletters (e.g., "AI‑Tool Stack" weekly).

## First 100 Users Strategy
1. **Beta Invite Loop** – Reach out to 30 contacts from the founder network, offering a free audit in exchange for feedback.
2. **Community AMA** – Host a live AMA on Indie Hackers and r/MachineLearning, demo the shareable audit link and collect sign‑ups.
3. **Referral Bonus** – Each user who shares a successful audit link gives both parties a $10 credit toward a premium report.
4. **Content Seeding** – Publish a concise case‑study titled *"How a 12‑engineer startup saved $1,200/mo with LedgerAI"* on Medium and LinkedIn, linking to the live demo.

## Unfair Distribution Channel
- **Partner Integration**: Offer LedgerAI as a free add‑on for the *Cursor* and *GitHub Copilot* onboarding flows. By embedding a one‑click audit link inside their extension UI, we tap into thousands of active users without traditional marketing spend.
